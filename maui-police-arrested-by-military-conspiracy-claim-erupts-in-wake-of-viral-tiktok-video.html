    Maui Police Arrested By Military Conspiracy Claim Erupts In Wake Of Viral Tiktok Video

    The reports of Maui police being arrested by ex-special US military forces have sparked significant intrigue and discussions across social media platforms. This unusual claim has generated widespread attention, with many seeking to understand the circumstances surrounding this event.

    24 Jul 2024, 00:00

    The reports of Maui police being arrested by ex-special US military forces have sparked significant intrigue and discussions across social media platforms.

    This unusual claim has generated widespread attention, with many seeking to understand the circumstances surrounding this event.

    The saga began when a TikToker, known as @peterbrent_72, posted a video in which he asserted that former elite members of the US military had detained Maui’s local police officers.

    According to his account, these arrests were made on charges of treason against the nation. The TikTok video quickly went viral, causing a sensation online.

    In the video, the TikToker described the individuals responsible for the arrests as “ex-military elite special force members.”

    He went on to claim that these “special force” operatives had taken control of the docks, where they confronted the local police officers.

    Reportedly armed with rifles, they demanded that the local law enforcement officers lower their weapons, after which they proceeded to arrest them.

    One of the noteworthy aspects of this claim is TikToker’s assertion that these individuals were not acting under the auspices of the US military but were, in fact, ex-military personnel.

    He further stated that some of them identified themselves as ex-Navy SEALs and rangers, which were distinguishable by the patches and insignias on their uniforms.

    Interestingly, the TikToker suggested that these ex-commanders had conveyed their presence in Maui as an effort to assist and protect the local citizens. This aspect of the claim has raised questions about their intentions and motivations.

    As with many viral claims on social media, it’s important to exercise caution and scepticism until official sources or authorities provide clarification on the situation.

    As of now, the veracity of this claim remains uncertain, and the circumstances surrounding the alleged arrests of Maui police officers remain shrouded in mystery.

    What Happened?

    As reported by KITV 4, a contingent of military personnel has been deployed to the island to collaborate with local law enforcement agencies.

    Their mission encompasses a range of critical tasks, including aiding in search and recovery efforts, securing restricted areas, assisting in the distribution of donations, and offering support to survivors in the wake of the devastating Maui-Lahaina wildfires.

    This deployment became necessary due to reported staffing shortages within the local police department.

    According to Lt. Nick Krau, who chairs the State of Hawaii Organization of Police Officers, the current number of officers falls below 400, highlighting the pressing need for additional support.

    Colonel Hatcher, the Deputy Commander of the Joint Task Force 50, which comprises military personnel and National Guard members, expressed gratitude for the positive reception they have received from the public.

    He noted that the majority of the Lahaina community has been appreciative of the soldiers’ efforts and extends their gratitude for their service.

    The tragic Maui-Lahaina wildfires, which ignited on August 8, have ravaged an extensive area, consuming 2,170 acres of land.

    This catastrophic event has claimed the lives of over 100 individuals and laid waste to more than 2,000 buildings, leaving many residents without homes and facing immense challenges in its aftermath.

    Who was arrested?

    It was claimed by TikToker @peterbrent_72 that local police officers in Maui were arrested by individuals described as “ex-military elite special force members.” However, this information was not confirmed by official news sources or authorities.
