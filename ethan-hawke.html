    Ethan Green Hawke (born on November 6, 1970) in Austin, Texas, U.S. He is an American actor, filmmaker, and screenwriter.

    Career

    He has received nominations for four Academy Awards and a Tony Award. As a director, he released three feature films, three off-Broadway plays, and a film. As a writer, he has also written three novels and one graphic novel. He began his acting career with a leading role Benjamin “Ben” Crandall in the science fiction feature Explorers (1985). He made a breakthrough appearance in the drama film Dead Poets Society (1989).

    Hawke appeared in various films before taking a role in the comedy-drama film Reality Bites (1994) as Troy Dyer, for which he received critical praise. He starred in the trilogy: Before Sunrise (1995), Before Sunset (2004), Before Midnight (2013), alongside Julie Delpy, all of which received critical praise.

    He earned recognition for his writing contributions to Before Sunset and Before Midnight. Hawke has received nominations twice for both in 2005 and 2015 for the Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay and the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. In 2019, he won the Independent Spirit Awards for Best Male Lead for his performance in the First Reformed (2017).

    Hawke starred in the epic coming-of-age drama film Boyhood (2014), for which he received further honor and received nominations. In 2021, he received nominations for TCA Awards for Individual Achievement in Drama for his performance in the historical drama The Good Lord Bird (2020).
