    Lydia Jacoby- Biography

    Lydia Jacoby is a competitive swimmer from the United States who specializes in breaststroke and solo medley events. She is well-known for the efforts she put in from a young age to achieve her goals. She just made history and became a national celebrity after winning a gold medal in the 100m Breaststroke race at the Tokyo Olympics 2020.

    Lydia Jacoby- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

    Lydia Alice Jacoby was born on February 29, 2004, in Anchorage, Alaska, and raised in Seward, Alaska. She began her career at the age of six when she joined the Seward Tsunami Swim Club, a local swimming club. Jacoby set her first Alaska state swimming record when she was 12 years old. Jacoby attended Seward High School in Seward, where she was a member of the swim team and set state records in the 100-yard breaststroke in both 2018 and 2019. She elected to be home-schooled rather than compete on the school swim team due to the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. Jacoby plans to return to Seward High School in the fall of 2021 for her senior year.

    Jacoby was a member of the Snow River String Band for six years, a bluegrass band that performed multiple times at the Anchorage Folk Festival. Aside from swimming, she can also play guitar and piano, as well as bass and sing. She was also a high school newspaper columnist and has appeared in several Port City Players productions. Lydia enrolled at the University of Texas at Austin with the intention of competing in swimming competitions. She was interested in majoring in fashion design when she first joined.
