    Tony Khan provides an update on Ric Flair regarding his AEW contract.

    WWE Hall of Famer Ric Flair made his debut on the October 25 episode of AEW Dynamite. There, he was revealed as Tony Khan’s gift for Sting. On November 2, AEW announced that Flair had signed a multi-year deal with the company, including a sponsorship deal for Wooooo! Energy.

    Speaking with Joseph Staszewski of the New York Post, Tony Khan commented on bringing Flair into AEW. He noted that the legend came with a “high-paying sponsorship.” Khan also stated that the company is not paying Flair; instead, the company is getting revenue from Wooooo! Energy.

    “We’re not paying Ric Flair; Ric Flair is essentially paying us,” Khan said. “We’re getting paid by Wooooo Energy for all of his appearances, so we’re collecting revenue from them.”

    Khan also noted that Ric Flair’s involvement did not influence the timeline of Sting’s final chapter, and it meant a lot that Flair was brought along for the ride, as “The Icon” is very loyal to him.

    Ric Flair: Some People In WWE Were Upset I Signed With AEW

    Ric Flair recently stated that some people in WWE were upset that he signed with AEW. Speaking on Shannon Sharpe on Club Shay Shay, he shared his thoughts on the reaction.

    “I think some people are upset with me now that I went to work at AEW. I don’t know why. If you’re acknowledging, and you think I’ve contributed to the business, and I know I have because I hear it every day, you would think they would let me do whatever I want to do. As long as it doesn’t affect them. I’m not going to get on TV and say ‘AEW is better,’ I’m not doing something stupid. I’m just working. That’s all I know how to do. They’re mad. Not mad, but they did something…I’ll tell you off camera.”
